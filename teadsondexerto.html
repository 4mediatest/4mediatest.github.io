Sledgehammer Reddit AMA Reveals Details on League Play and Esports

by Sparx  @Spaarx_

Sledgehammer recently did an Ask Me Anything (AMA) on Reddit in which they answered some of the community’s questions about Call of Duty: WWII.

Though the team didn’t go into massive detail on too many areas, there were a few pieces of interesting information released during the AMA. We’ve summarized some of the key points and pieces of information revealed by the Sledgehammer team.

x

Advertisement

Supply Drops will contain only cosmetic variants to weapons.

In recent titles, players have been able to unlock weapon variants through Supply Drops with different stats to the standard weapons. Invariably, certain variants proved more powerful, at times giving the game an unfortunate “pay to win” feel in which those who spent more money on Supply Drops and unlocked weapon variants might have an in-game advantage over those who chose not to. This will no longer be the case for WWII.

 

Ranked Play and GameBattles are important components of the WWII feature set.

For competitive players and fans, this might be the biggest news coming from the Sledgehammer AMA. Call of Duty has lacked a coherent ranked system since Black Ops 2, so the return of a ranked play system alongside the recent addition of GameBattles integration is welcome news. The description by Michael Condrey of the features as “important components” is also encouraging, and Condrey also stated that more competitive news would be coming later this summer.

x

Advertisement

Sledgehammer are “very dedicated” to the esports scene.

In response to another question about Sledgehammer’s commitment to esports for Call of Duty, Michael Condrey stated that the team were working closely with MLG and the players to figure out the best combination of maps and modes.

 

Good news for PC players, bad news for Nintendo fans

Sledgehammer stated that the team were “big PC gamers too and are passionate about making that a good experience”, which will hopefully result in a better experience on a platform for which Call of Duty games have occasionally been lackluster. Details on the PC version are apparently coming soon. Fans of the Nintendo Switch, however, will not be able to play the game on Nintendo’s newest console.

x

Advertisement

Score streak progress will reset on death by default

In simpler times, streaks could only be earned by amassing kills or score across a single life – death would put the counter back to zero. In some more recent titles, however, there have been options or scenarios in which streaks can be maintained through death. For WWII, it seems that for most cases the classic approach will be taken, although there will be a Basic Training perk that allows streaks to persist through death, but at a higher cost to earn.
